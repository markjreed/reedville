WHATL Computus: Calendar Chart

This shows the letter and number assignments to each day in the year. In any given year, each day whose letter matches the year's κυριακαίο γράμματο ("dominical letter") is a Κυριακό (Sunday), and each day whose number matches the epact is an ecclesiastical new moon.

For example, for the year 2014 (which corresponds to 2010 AD), the epact is 6 and the κυριακαίο γράμματο is Δ.

To find Easter, most of the table is not needed, of course. The earliest possible date for Easter is 28 Δύστρο, and the lateset possible date is 1 Αρτεμίσιο. The procedure is as follows:

  1. Determine the numeric label of the ecclesiastical full moons, which occur 14 days after the new moons. For 2014, the epact of 6 means that the full moons fall on days numbered 20.
  2. Find the first day with that number on or after the ecclesiastical vernal equinox (27 Δύστρο, highlighted in green). This is the day of the Paschal Moon; for 2014, that's the 7th day of Ξανθικό.
  3. Starting with the day after the full moon, find the first Κυριακό That is Easter. In 2014, that's the 10th of Ξανθικό.
