Okaikiar Sounds

Home Mark's Page Mark's Conlangs Okaikiar

Okaikiar has a relatively small set of phonemes: seven consonants and six vowels. These combine into a total of 864 possible syllables. The native writing system, called Orendiar, has distinct shapes for each phoneme, which are combined to form symbols for each of these syllables. Most of the time on these pages I use the Roman transcription system given below, but you can see the Orendiar version of the word Okaikiar in the background image.

Vowels

The six vowels, on the other hand, are represented by the five standard Roman vowel letters plus apostrophe, so the transcription doesn't always agree with IPA. Here are the vowels with four transcriptions: standard, Unicode IPA, X-SAMPA, and the modified version of X-SAMPA used on the conlang mailing list.
